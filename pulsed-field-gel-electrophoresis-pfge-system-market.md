<p><p>The Pulsed Field Gel Electrophoresis (PFGE) market comprises various technologies designed for separating large DNA fragments. Rotating Gel Electrophoresis (RGE) utilizes rotational forces for enhanced separation. Field-Inversion Gel Electrophoresis (FIGE) periodically reverses the electric field to improve resolution. Transverse-Alternating Field Gel Electrophoresis (TAFE) uses alternating fields in multiple directions. Contour-Clamped Homogeneous Electric Fields (CHEF) allows precise manipulation of fields for uniform separation. Orthogonal-Field Alternation Gel Electrophoresis (OFAGE) employs orthogonal fields, while Programmable Autonomously-Controlled Electrodes (PACE) and Pulsed-Homogeneous Orthogonal Field Gel Electrophoresis (PHOGE) offer advanced control for optimized separations.</p></p>
