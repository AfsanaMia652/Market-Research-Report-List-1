<p><p>The stainless steel suture market features two primary types: monofilament and braided sutures. Monofilament sutures consist of a single, solid strand, offering reduced tissue trauma and minimal friction, making them ideal for delicate procedures. In contrast, braided sutures are composed of multiple strands woven together, providing enhanced strength and knot security, which is beneficial for high-tension areas. Both types are widely used in surgical applications, each catering to specific clinical needs and preferences based on their unique properties.</p></p>
