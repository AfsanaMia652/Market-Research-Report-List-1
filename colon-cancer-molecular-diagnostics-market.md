<p><p>The colon cancer molecular diagnostics market comprises various technologies for detecting genetic abnormalities in tumors. PCR (Polymerase Chain Reaction) amplifies specific DNA sequences, enabling precise mutation detection. FISH (Fluorescence In Situ Hybridization) visualizes chromosomal alterations by using fluorescent probes. DNA sequencing provides detailed insights into the genetic makeup of cancer cells, revealing mutations and variations. Gene chips, or microarrays, allow for the simultaneous analysis of thousands of genes, identifying gene expression patterns associated with colon cancer progression and treatment response.</p></p>
