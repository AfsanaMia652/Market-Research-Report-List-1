<p><p>The fluorocarbon surfactants market is segmented into four main types: amphoteric, anionic, cationic, and nonionic. Amphoteric surfactants possess both positive and negative charges, offering versatility in various applications. Anionic surfactants carry a negative charge, making them effective in cleaning and emulsifying. Cationic surfactants have a positive charge, known for their antibacterial properties and ability to condition surfaces. Nonionic surfactants are neutral and are widely used for their stability and compatibility in formulations, particularly in cosmetics and industrial applications.</p></p>
