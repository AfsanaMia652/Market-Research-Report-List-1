<p><p>The DNA modifying agents market encompasses various types, including cross-linkers and enzymes. Cross-linkers are chemicals that facilitate the formation of covalent bonds between DNA strands, impacting replication and repair, essential for cancer therapies and genetic research. Enzymes, such as restriction endonucleases and ligases, manipulate DNA by cutting and joining strands, playing a vital role in biotechnology, gene editing, and synthetic biology. Together, these agents drive advancements in molecular biology and therapeutic development, enhancing our understanding of genetic material.</p></p>
