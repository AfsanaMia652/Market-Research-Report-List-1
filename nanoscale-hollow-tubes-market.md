<p><p>The nanoscale hollow tubes market is primarily categorized into single-walled and multi-walled nanotubes. Single-walled nanotubes (SWCNTs) consist of a single layer of carbon atoms rolled into a cylindrical shape, offering unique electrical and mechanical properties. Multi-walled nanotubes (MWCNTs), on the other hand, consist of multiple concentric layers of carbon, enhancing strength and stability. Both types have diverse applications in electronics, materials science, and biomedical fields, catering to varying requirements for conductivity, weight, and structural integrity.</p></p>
