<p><p>The enteral feeding catheter market includes several key types: gastrostomy tubes, nasoenteric tubes, and other specialized devices. Gastrostomy tubes are surgically placed directly into the stomach for long-term feeding, ideal for patients with swallowing difficulties. Nasoenteric tubes, on the other hand, are inserted through the nose and extend to the small intestine, suitable for short-term use. Other market types encompass devices like jejunostomy tubes and customized feeding solutions, catering to specific patient needs and varying conditions.</p></p>
