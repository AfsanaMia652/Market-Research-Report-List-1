<p><p>The marine composites market comprises several key types, each with distinct properties and applications. Glass Fiber Reinforced Plastics (GFRPs) are widely used for their excellent strength-to-weight ratio and corrosion resistance, making them suitable for hulls and decks. Polymer Fiber Reinforced Plastics (PFRPs) offer versatility in design and can be engineered for specific marine uses. Carbon Fiber Reinforced Plastics (CFRPs) provide superior strength and stiffness, ideal for high-performance applications like racing yachts. Other materials include aramid composites and hybrid systems, addressing niche requirements in marine engineering.</p></p>
